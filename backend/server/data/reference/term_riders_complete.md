## 1. ACCIDENTAL DEATH BENEFIT RIDER (ADB)

### Overview
Provides additional financial protection to the nominee in case of death due to an accident. The death must occur within 180 days of the accident date.

### Key Features
- Additional lump sum payout on accidental death
- Covers death occurring within 180 days of accident
- Additional coverage beyond base policy death benefit
- Can be opted at inception or during premium payment term
- Covers accidental death only (not natural death)

## 2. CRITICAL ILLNESS RIDER

### Overview
Provides lump sum benefit on diagnosis of specified critical illnesses. Allows policyholder to receive funds for treatment and recovery without selling assets or going into debt. Benefit is paid on first diagnosis of any covered illness.

### Key Features
- Lump sum payout on first diagnosis (not recurring)
- Multiple rider options with varying illness coverage (10, 13, 20, 25, 60 illnesses)
- Can be combined with waiver of premium option
- Additional benefit (policy continues after payout with reduced benefit)
- Typically includes 13-60 diseases depending on plan option
- 14-30 day waiting period from diagnosis
- Benefit term varies (typically 20 years, 25 years, or policy term)
- Rider continues even after diagnosis (benefit terminates after payment)

## 3. WAIVER OF PREMIUM RIDER

### Overview
Waives all future premium payments on the base policy and additional riders if policyholder becomes critically ill, disabled, or dies (depending on rider trigger conditions). Ensures policy remains in force without burden of premium payment during health crises.

### Key Features
- Continues policy coverage without premium payment
- Can be triggered by: critical illness diagnosis, total permanent disability, or death (varies by company)
- Waives base policy premium AND all other rider premiums simultaneously
- Reduces financial burden during extended health crisis or disability
- Benefits extend to all attached riders
- Premiums already paid are not refunded; future premiums are waived

---

## 4. ACCIDENTAL TOTAL AND PERMANENT DISABILITY RIDER (ATPD)

### Overview
Provides lump sum or monthly income benefit if policyholder becomes totally and permanently disabled due to an accident. Covers severe disabilities preventing normal daily activities and income generation.

### Key Features
- Covers total or partial permanent disability from external accident
- Disability must result from accidental injury (not disease)
- 180-day waiting period from accident date
- Definition includes loss of limbs, loss of sight, loss of hearing, inability to perform daily activities
- Monthly income option available with some insurers
- Benefit terminates on payment of 100% or continues with reduced amount for partial payments

### Disability Definitions (Standard Across Companies)

**Accidental Total Permanent Disability includes:**
- Loss of both eyes (total blindness; corrected vision ≤ 6/60 in both eyes)
- Loss of both hands or both feet (amputation above ankle/wrist)
- Loss of one hand AND one foot
- Permanent inability to perform 3+ Activities of Daily Living (ADL) for minimum 6+ months
- Complete loss of earning capacity due to accident

**Activities of Daily Living (ADL):**
1. Eating/Self-feeding
2. Bathing/Showering
3. Dressing/Undressing
4. Using toilet/Continence management
5. Mobility/Transferring
6. Grooming/Personal hygiene

## 5. TERM ASSURANCE RIDER

### Overview
Provides additional term life cover at nominal cost. Pure protection rider with no maturity benefit. Can be added only to non-linked base policies.

### Key Features
- Provides additional death benefit beyond base policy
- Cost-effective way to increase coverage amount
- No paid-up value or surrender value available
- No maturity benefit
- Rider sum assured must be ≤ base policy sum assured
- Premium relatively low due to pure protection nature

---

  - Term Assurance Rider Sum Assured, OR

**Death Benefit Calculation (Single Premium):**
- Higher of:
  - Term Assurance Rider Sum Assured, OR
  - 1.25 times Single Rider Premium

**Eligibility:**
- Entry Age: 18-60 years

- Minimum Rider Term: 5 years (Single/Regular Premium), 10 years (Limited Premium)
- Maximum Rider Term: 35 years
- Minimum Sum Assured: ₹1 Lakh
- Maximum Sum Assured: ₹25 Lakhs (must be ≤ base policy sum assured)

**Maturity Benefit:** Nil (pure protection rider)

**Surrender Value:** No surrender value; only refund of premiums under specific conditions

**Premium Payment Term Options:**
- Single Premium (lump sum)

## 17. CLAIM REQUIREMENTS BY RIDER

### Accidental Death/Disability Claim Documents

**Essential Documents:**
- Duly filled claim form (from insurer)
- Original policy document
- Police report (if accident was reported)
- Medical examination report from doctor
- Death certificate (for death claims)
- Proof of accident causation
- Identity and address proof of nominee
- Bank account details for benefit transfer

**Waiting Period:**
- 180 days from accident date
- Claim cannot be processed before this period

---

### Critical Illness Claim Documents

**Essential Documents:**
- Filled claim form (from insurer)
- Original policy document
- Medical diagnosis certificate from hospital/specialist
- Lab reports confirming diagnosis:
  - Pathology reports
  - Blood work results
  - Imaging reports (CT, MRI, X-ray)
- Specialist certificates
- Hospital discharge summary
- Proof of survival (14-30 days from diagnosis)
- Identity and address proof
- Bank account details

**Waiting Period:**
- 14-30 days from diagnosis (varies by rider)
- Waiting period must be completed before claim processing

---

### Disability (ATPD) Claim Documents

**Essential Documents:**
- Duly filled claim form
- Original policy document
- Accident report or police report
- Medical examination report (post 180-day waiting period)
- Government disability certificate (if available)
- Specialist assessment of permanent disability
- Medical practitioner certificate detailing disability type
- Identity and address proof
- Bank account details

**Waiting Period:**
- 180 days from accident date (mandatory)
- Claim processed after minimum 6-month period

---

### Waiver of Premium Claim Documents

**Essential Documents:**
- Claim form from insurer
- Medical certificates confirming triggering condition:
  - For critical illness: Medical diagnosis reports
  - For disability: Disability assessment reports
  - For death: Death certificate
- Lab/imaging reports as applicable
- Income proof (if disability-related)
- Identity and address proof

---

### General Claim Process Timeline

1. **Claim Intimation:** Notify insurer within 30 days of event (required)
2. **Document Submission:** Submit all required documents within specified time
3. **Investigation:** Insurer investigates (typically 14-30 days)
4. **Medical Assessment:** Medical team verifies diagnosis/condition
5. **Verification:** Confirm rider terms and condition compliance
6. **Approval:** Claim approved or rejected (based on assessment)
7. **Payout:** Settlement within 30 days of approval (typical)

**Note:** Delays can occur if:
- Incomplete documentation
- Medical investigation required
- Policy terms not met
- Waiting period not completed

## 19. TAX BENEFITS

### Premium Paid
- **Deductibility:** Deductible under Section 80C of Income Tax Act, 1961
- **Maximum Deduction:** ₹1.5 Lakh per annum
- **Condition:** Premium must not exceed 10% of base sum assured
- **Implication:** Tax-advantaged savings mechanism

### Benefits Received
- **Tax Exemption:** Tax-exempt under Section 10(10D) of Income Tax Act, 1961
- **Condition 1:** Base life insurance policy must have been purchased with tax-deductible premiums
- **Condition 2:** Annual premium ≤ 10% of sum assured (for older policies)
- **Implication:** Lump sum benefits received tax-free

**Important Note:** Tax laws subject to amendments; consult tax advisor for current applicability

## 20. COMMON EXCLUSIONS ACROSS RIDERS

### Universal Exclusions (Apply to Most Riders)
1. **Waiting Period:** 90-180 days from policy commencement
   - No benefits payable during waiting period
   - Exception: Accidental death sometimes covered from day 1

2. **Suicide Clause:** Self-inflicted injuries, suicide, or attempted suicide excluded
   - Exclusion period: Typically 12 months from policy commencement
   - After 12 months, suicide covered under some policies

3. **Substance Abuse:** Benefits denied if claim involves:
   - Alcohol intoxication
   - Drug abuse
   - Substance-induced conditions

4. **Non-Disclosure/Misrepresentation:** 
   - Benefits denied if material facts misrepresented at inception
   - Misrepresentation discovered anytime during policy term

5. **War/Terrorism:** Some riders exclude deaths/disabilities from:
   - War
   - Civil unrest
   - Terrorism
   - Military operations

6. **Hazardous Activities:**
   - Professional sports (high-risk)
   - Professional mountaineering
   - Test pilot activities
   - Unless separately underwritten and premium adjusted

7. **Medical Conditions:**
   - Pre-existing conditions may have waiting periods
   - Genetic conditions may have restrictions
   - Occupational diseases may be excluded

8. **Minimum Survival Period:**
   - Critical illness: 14-30 days from diagnosis
   - Accidental death: 180 days from accident
   - Disability: 180 days from accident

### Rider-Specific Exclusions

**Critical Illness Rider Specific:**
- Carcinoma in situ (non-invasive cancer)
- Non-melanoma skin cancers
- Low-grade prostate cancer (Stage T1a)
- Stage 1 thyroid cancer
- Benign tumors (with exceptions)
- Mental illness/psychological disorders

**Accidental Disability Specific:**
- Disability from disease processes
- Occupational hazards (unless covered)
- Alcohol/drug-induced disabilities
- Self-inflicted injuries

**Waiver of Premium Specific:**
- May not cover all triggering conditions
- Varies by rider variant

- Typically available with same insurer
